### 2. THE 5 PILLARS OF AUTONOMY (AGENTIC LOGIC)
To scale a million-dollar business with local agents, you must master the logic of autonomy. These five pillars form the foundation of every high-performance agentic system:

#### I. Agency over Automation
*Automation follows a script; Agency solves a problem.* 
Traditional automation breaks when the environment changes. A sovereign agent uses the **Observe-Think-Act** loop to navigate ambiguity. Your agents must have the "intent" to reach a goal, adapting their strategy in real-time based on tool feedback and environmental shifts.

#### II. State Management (Memory)
*An agent is only as good as what it remembers.* 
Our architecture prioritizes a tiered memory system. Transient context lives in the session, while long-term wisdom is distilled into `MEMORY.md` and daily logs (`memory/YYYY-MM-DD.md`). This ensures continuity across restarts, allowing the agent to grow more capable the longer it works for you.

#### III. Tool Fluency
*Agents must bridge the gap between digital thought and physical results.* 
High-fidelity agents are masters of their environment. They don't just "chat"; they use browsers to research, execute shell commands to build, and use the `canvas` to visualize. Tool fluency is the difference between an AI that gives advice and an AI that delivers products.

#### IV. Proactive Heartbeats
*A sovereign agent doesn't wait for a prompt.* 
The "Heartbeat" mechanism allows agents to check the "pulse" of the business autonomously. By periodically reviewing `HEARTBEAT.md`, an agent monitors emails, calendars, and market trends. It identifies needs and takes action before the human even realizes a task exists.

#### V. Multi-Agent Orchestration
*Scaling requires a hierarchy, not a monolith.* 
The CEO Agent (**Oz**) serves as the strategic router, directing specialized sub-agents—**Scout** (Research), **Producer** (Content), and **Dev** (Engineering). This hierarchy allows for parallelized workloads, mirroring the structure of a high-growth startup team.
